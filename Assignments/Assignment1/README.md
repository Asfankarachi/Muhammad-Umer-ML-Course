# Assignment 1 — AI Ethics and Bias Analysis

**Course:** ITAI 4376 — AI Applications
**Instructor:** Professor Patricia McManus
**Student:** Muhammad Umer
**Institution:** Houston City College | Spring 2026

## Problem Statement

This assignment examined the ethical challenges associated with deploying artificial intelligence systems, with particular attention to algorithmic bias, fairness, and accountability.

## Approach and Methods

I identified the primary sources of bias in machine learning pipelines, including biased training data and flawed feature selection. I reviewed documented cases of AI bias in hiring algorithms, facial recognition systems, and clinical decision support tools.

## Results and Interpretation

Algorithmic bias is not an isolated technical problem but a systemic issue rooted in data collection practices and institutional structures. Addressing it requires both technical interventions and organizational commitments to inclusive development practices.

## Key Takeaway

Ethical AI development is not optional. As AI systems become more embedded in consequential decisions, identifying and mitigating bias becomes a core professional obligation.
