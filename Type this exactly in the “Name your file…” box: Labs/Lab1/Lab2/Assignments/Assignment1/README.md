# Assignment 1 — AI Ethics and Bias Analysis

**Course:** ITAI 4376 — AI Applications  
**Student:** Muhammad Umer  
**Semester:** Spring 2026

## Problem Statement

This assignment examined how algorithmic bias enters AI systems and what its consequences are when those systems are deployed in high-stakes domains such as healthcare, hiring, and criminal justice.

## Approach and Methods

I analyzed documented cases of biased AI systems and identified the stages in the development pipeline where bias was introduced, whether through unrepresentative training data, flawed evaluation practices, or lack of diverse stakeholder input during design. I then applied ethical frameworks including fairness, accountability, and transparency to evaluate each case.

## Results and What They Mean

The analysis revealed that bias in AI is rarely the result of a single technical failure. It typically emerges from a combination of data gaps, underrepresentation of affected populations in the design process, and the absence of ongoing monitoring after deployment. Addressing it requires both technical solutions and institutional accountability structures that hold developers and deploying organizations responsible for outcomes.
